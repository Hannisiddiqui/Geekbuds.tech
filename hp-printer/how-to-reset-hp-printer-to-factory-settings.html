How to Reset HP Printer to Factory Settings: A Complete Guide for Every User

If you’re facing issues with your HP printer—like connectivity problems, error codes, or stubborn print queues—knowing how to reset HP printer to factory settings can save you time and effort. A factory reset is one of the simplest ways to restore your printer’s functionality by clearing out errors and resetting its configurations. This guide will walk you through several effective reset methods, including using the control panel, HP Smart App, and the Embedded Web Server (EWS). No matter your HP printer model, these steps will help get your printer running smoothly again.

Understanding HP Printer Resets: What to Expect

Learning how to reset HP printer to factory settings can be invaluable when troubleshooting frequent issues. Let’s start with the basics.

What is a Factory Reset on an HP Printer?

A factory reset is a hard reset process that erases custom configurations, Wi-Fi connections, and temporary files, restoring the printer to its out-of-box state. Whether you're dealing with an error code or want to set up your printer on a new network, knowing how to reset HP printer to factory settings is helpful for various situations.

When to Reset Your HP Printer?

  • Persistent Errors: If you frequently see error messages, a factory reset can clear these.
  • Wi-Fi Connection Issues: Restores network settings, allowing fresh Wi-Fi setup.
  • Preparation for a New Network: Setting up on a different network requires reset to clear old credentials.
  • Print Queue Errors: Resolving issues in the queue is easier with a reset.
  • Setting Up New Ink Cartridges: Resetting may help when setting up new or third-party ink cartridges.

Four Methods to Reset HP Printer to Factory Settings

We’ll cover four ways on how to reset HP printer to factory settings, each tailored to different printer models.

Method 1: Soft Reset for HP Printers

A soft reset is the most straightforward option and typically fixes minor issues without affecting custom settings.

Steps to Perform a Soft Reset:

  1. Turn off your printer and unplug the power cable from both the wall and the printer.
  2. Wait at least 60 seconds to allow any remaining power to drain.
  3. Reconnect the power cable to both the wall outlet and the printer.
  4. Turn on the printer and check if the issue persists.

Quick Tip: This method of how to reset HP printer to factory settings is ideal for minor issues like connectivity drops or temporary error codes. If problems continue, proceed with a hard reset.

Method 2: Factory Reset via Printer Control Panel

Most HP printers offer a factory reset option via the control panel. The steps vary depending on whether your printer has a touchscreen.

For Printers with a Touchscreen:

  1. On the home screen, open the Setup or Menu option.
  2. Go to Tools or Printer Maintenance settings.
  3. Select Restore Factory Defaults and confirm by choosing Yes.

For Printers with a Touchscreen:

  1. Turn off the printer and disconnect the power cord.
  2. Press and hold the Resume button.
  3. While holding the Resume button, reconnect the printer to power.
  4. Release the button once the printer lights flash.

Why This Works: Resetting through the control panel is a reliable method in understanding how to reset HP printer to factory settings and is effective for clearing custom configurations.

Method 3: Resetting Using the Embedded Web Server (EWS)

The Embedded Web Server (EWS) is a helpful tool for users comfortable with accessing printer settings through a browser.

Steps to Reset Through EWS:

  1. Find the printer’s IP address on the control panel (usually under network settings or Wi-Fi settings).
  2. Enter the IP address into a web browser to open the Embedded Web Server.
  3. Navigate to the General tab and select Reset Factory Settings.
  4. Confirm the reset and wait for the printer to restart.

Note: This method is an essential step in how to reset HP printer to factory settings as it provides a comprehensive way to refresh settings using a computer.

Method 4: Using the HP Smart App to Reset Remotely

For those who prefer remote options, the HP Smart App offers an easy way to reset HP printers.

Steps to Reset HP Printer Using HP Smart App:

  1. Open the HP Smart App on your smartphone or tablet.
  2. Select your printer and navigate to Settings > Advanced Settings.
  3. Under Restore Defaults, select Factory Reset and confirm.

Tip: This method makes how to reset HP printer to factory settings accessible for users who may not be near their printer.

Important Things to Remember Before Resetting

Before performing a reset, consider these important factors to avoid losing crucial settings.

  • Backup Important Settings: Save custom network information or print settings that might be erased during a reset.
  • Understand Potential Data Loss: A hard reset will clear Wi-Fi credentials, custom setups, and other personal data.

By understanding these precautions, you can use how to reset HP printer to factory settings without unexpected data loss.

Troubleshooting Common Issues After a Reset

After performing how to reset HP printer to factory settings, some minor issues may still arise. Here’s how to handle them:

  • Reconnecting to Wi-Fi: After resetting, you’ll need to re-enter your network information. Use the Wi-Fi setup option on the control panel or the HP Smart App.
  • Updating Printer Drivers: To ensure optimal performance, make sure your printer drivers are up-to-date. This can usually be done through your computer’s Device Manager or by downloading drivers from the HP website.
  • Clearing Print Queue Errors: Sometimes a print queue can get backed up, even after resetting. Open the print queue on your computer, clear pending jobs, and try printing again.
