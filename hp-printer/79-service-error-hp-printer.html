79 Service Error HP Printer – Causes, Fixes, and Professional Repair Services

At Geekbuds, we understand how frustrating it can be when your HP printer displays the 79 Service Error and suddenly stops working. This error is common across various HP LaserJet models and is typically related to firmware issues, corrupted print jobs, or faulty hardware components.

In this guide, we’ll explain what 79 Service Error HP Printer means, its causes, troubleshooting steps, and how Geekbuds' professional printer repair services can help you resolve it quickly.

What is the 79 Service Error on an HP Printer?

The 79 Service Error HP Printer usually occurs due to a firmware issue or a communication problem between the printer and the network or computer. It often results in the printer freezing, displaying an error message, or refusing to complete print jobs.

This error can appear as:

  • "79 Service Error. Turn off then on."
  • "79.00FE Service Error."
  • "79 Error - Printer Restart Required."

If your HP printer is stuck with this error message, don’t worry. Below, we’ll explore why this happens and how you can fix it.

Common Causes of 79 Service Error HP Printer

Several factors can trigger the 79 Service Error HP Printer issue. The most common causes include:

  1. Outdated or Corrupt Firmware
    HP regularly releases firmware updates to fix bugs and improve printer functionality. If your printer’s firmware is outdated or corrupted, it can lead to 79 service errors.
  2. Problematic Print Jobs
    A corrupt or incompatible print job sent from a computer can cause the printer to crash and display this error.
  3. Faulty Network Connection
    If the printer is connected via Wi-Fi or Ethernet, network issues or conflicts can result in 79 Service Error HP Printer problems.
  4. Malfunctioning DIMM or Hardware Issues
    In some cases, a faulty DIMM (memory module) or other internal hardware component may be causing the error.
  5. Third-Party Plugins or Drivers
    Some non-HP drivers, plugins, or software installed on your system may interfere with the printer’s normal functioning.

How to Fix 79 Service Error HP Printer

If you’re experiencing a 79 Service Error HP Printer, follow these troubleshooting steps to resolve the issue.

Step 1: Restart the Printer

  • Turn off the printer.
  • Unplug the power cord and wait 30 seconds.
  • Plug the printer back in and power it on.
  • If the error persists, move to the next step.

Step 2: Remove Pending Print Jobs

  • On your computer, go to Control Panel > Devices and Printers.
  • Locate your HP printer and right-click on it.
  • Click See what’s printing and cancel all pending print jobs.
  • Restart the printer and try printing again.

Step 3: Update the Printer Firmware

  • Visit HP’s official website and navigate to the support section.
  • Enter your printer model and download the latest firmware update.
  • Install the firmware following the on-screen instructions.
  • Restart your printer and check if the error is resolved.

Step 4: Check Network and Cable Connections

  • If using Wi-Fi, restart your router and printer.
  • If using Ethernet, try replacing the cable or using a different port.
  • If the error occurs when printing via network, try a USB connection instead.

Step 5: Remove and Reinstall the Printer Driver

  • Go to Control Panel > Devices and Printers.
  • Right-click your printer and select Remove device.
  • Download the latest HP printer driver from HP’s website and install it.
  • Restart your computer and printer, then try printing again.

Step 6: Check Hardware Components

  • If you recently installed a new DIMM (memory module), remove it and restart the printer.
  • Check for loose cables or any physical damage inside the printer.
  • If the issue persists, you may need professional repair services.
