How to Fix 79 Service Error HP Printer: A Complete Troubleshooting Guide

Printers are an essential part of daily office and home operations, but encountering errors can be frustrating, especially when they disrupt productivity. One such common error faced by HP printer users is the 79 service error. This issue can prevent your printer from functioning properly, leading to delays and frustration.

If you are dealing with this error and searching for how to fix 79 service error HP printer, this comprehensive guide will provide you with all the necessary steps to troubleshoot and resolve the issue. Whether it is caused by a corrupt print job, outdated firmware, network issues, or hardware failure, we will walk you through different solutions in a step-by-step manner.

What is the 79 Service Error on an HP Printer?

The 79 service error is primarily a firmware-related issue that occurs in HP LaserJet and other HP printer models. It is usually caused by corrupt print jobs, outdated firmware, network problems, or failing hardware components.

When this error occurs, the printer may display the message "79 Service Error Turn Off Then On", or it may freeze, restart repeatedly, or become completely unresponsive. This error can be frustrating, especially when you need to print urgent documents.

Understanding the root cause of this error can help you apply the most effective solution and prevent it from happening in the future.

Common Causes of the 79 Service Error in HP Printers

Before diving into how to fix 79 service error HP printer, it’s important to identify what might be triggering the issue. The most common causes include:

  1. Corrupt Print Jobs – A print job with corrupt or incompatible data can cause the printer to crash and display the error message.
  2. Outdated or Corrupt Firmware – If the printer's firmware is outdated, it may not function properly, leading to service errors.
  3. Network Communication Issues – If the printer is connected to a network, conflicts or glitches in network communication may trigger the error.
  4. Faulty Hardware Components – Issues with the printer’s memory module (DIMM) or other internal components can lead to malfunctions.
  5. Third-Party Software Conflicts – Certain external applications or incompatible drivers can interfere with the printer's operations and cause service errors.

Now that you understand the potential causes, let’s move on to the solutions.

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Fix 79 Service Error HP Printer

If you encounter the 79 service error, follow these troubleshooting steps in the order given below to fix it effectively.

1. Power Cycle the Printer (Restart the Printer Properly)

The first and simplest step in troubleshooting 79 service error HP printer is performing a power cycle. Restarting the printer can clear temporary glitches and refresh the system.

  1. Press the Power button on your printer to turn it off.
  2. Unplug the power cable from both the printer and the wall outlet.
  3. Wait for at least 60 seconds to allow the printer’s internal memory to clear.
  4. Plug the power cable back in and turn on the printer.
  5. Check if the error message still appears.

If the error persists, move on to the next step.

2. Clear the Print Queue (Cancel All Print Jobs)

A corrupt print job sent to the printer can trigger the 79 service error. Clearing the print queue can resolve this issue.

For Windows Users:
  1. Press Windows + R, type control printers, and press Enter.
  2. Right-click on your HP printer and select See what’s printing.
  3. Click Printer in the menu and choose Cancel All Documents.
  4. Restart your printer and try printing again.
For macOS Users:
  1. Open System Preferences and go to Printers & Scanners.
  2. Select your HP printer and click Open Print Queue.
  3. Cancel any pending print jobs.
  4. Restart your printer and check if the error is resolved.

If the print queue was the issue, the error should no longer appear.

3. Update Printer Firmware (Fix Software Bugs and Improve Performance)

HP frequently releases firmware updates to fix known bugs, improve printer performance, and resolve issues like the 79 service error.

How to Update HP Printer Firmware:
  1. Visit the HP Support website.
  2. Enter your HP printer model and locate the latest firmware update.
  3. Download and install the firmware update using the HP update utility or the HP Smart App.
  4. Restart the printer and check if the error is resolved.

Firmware updates help eliminate software-related conflicts and improve printer performance.

4. Disconnect the Printer from the Network (Identify Network-Related Issues)

If your printer is connected to a wireless or Ethernet network, a communication error may be causing the 79 service error.

Steps to check network connectivity:
  1. Disconnect the printer from Wi-Fi or unplug the Ethernet cable.
  2. Restart the printer and check if it functions normally in offline mode.
  3. If the error disappears, the problem may be with the network connection.
  4. Try reconnecting to the network and see if the error returns.
  5. If the issue reappears after reconnecting, check your router settings, update network drivers, or consider using a direct USB connection.

5. Perform a Factory Reset (Restore Default Settings)

If your printer's settings have become corrupted, performing a factory reset may resolve the 79 service error.

How to Reset Your HP Printer:
  1. Open the Control Panel on your printer.
  2. Navigate to Setup > Reset > Restore Factory Defaults.
  3. Confirm the reset and allow the printer to restart.

Note: A factory reset will erase all custom settings, so you may need to reconfigure your printer after the reset.

6. Remove Any Third-Party Software or Drivers (Prevent Compatibility Issues)

Third-party software, drivers, or printer management applications can sometimes cause conflicts that lead to service errors.

How to Fix:
  1. Uninstall any third-party printer software installed on your computer.
  2. Reinstall the official HP drivers from the HP website.
  3. Restart your printer and check if the issue is resolved.

Using official HP drivers ensures compatibility and prevents software conflicts.

7. Check for Hardware Issues (Diagnose Memory and Internal Components)

If all software-related fixes fail, the 79 service error may be caused by a hardware issue.

  1. If your printer has additional DIMM memory modules, try removing them and restarting the printer.
  2. Inspect internal components for loose connections or physical damage.
  3. If the printer is under warranty, contact HP support for a hardware diagnosis.

If a hardware fault is detected, you may need professional printer repair services.

Preventing 79 Service Error in HP Printers

To prevent encountering the 79 service error again, follow these best practices:

  1. Regularly update your printer’s firmware to avoid software conflicts.
  2. Use only genuine HP drivers and software to maintain compatibility.
  3. Clear print queues frequently to remove stuck or corrupt jobs.
  4. Ensure stable network connectivity for network-based printers.
  5. Avoid printing from outdated or unsupported applications.

By following these preventive measures, you can maintain a smooth and error-free printing experience.
